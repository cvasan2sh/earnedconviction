THE FORGE — PROBLEM STATEMENT
earnedconviction.com | Growth + Product Strategy | March 2026
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CONTEXT

earnedconviction.com is live with zero audience. I have 90-120 days of
financial runway. This is a hard constraint, not a preference. My north
star is $100K ARR in B2B SaaS. earnedconviction.com is meant to serve
as the distribution channel, proof of thinking, and audience foundation
that makes that north star reachable.

I do not yet have a defined product. This is intentional — I am asking
The Forge to do two things in the same run: first, generate and evaluate
product ideas that are genuinely viable given my background and
constraints; second, determine whether earnedconviction.com as a
content and distribution platform can drive the right product to $100K
ARR within my runway.

These are not separate problems. The product choice and the distribution
strategy are load-bearing on each other. A product that cannot be
distributed through content and outbound in 90-120 days is the wrong
product regardless of its quality. A distribution strategy that does not
connect to a product with real buyer urgency is theater regardless of
its execution quality.


WHO I AM — STRUCTURAL ASSETS AND KNOWN LIABILITIES

Assets:
- 5+ years enterprise SaaS sales at Freshworks — I understand how
  mid-market and enterprise ops buyers think, evaluate, and commit.
  I know what makes them move fast and what makes them stall.
- Strong abstract reasoning and cross-domain thinking — I can hold
  complex problems and find non-obvious connections.
- The Forge — a multi-agent deliberation system I designed and built.
  It is running locally. It is real, not conceptual.
- earnedconviction.com — live, designed with a specific philosophy:
  feels like the best book you ever read designed by Apple. Warm,
  serious, rewards slow reading. Built on Next.js, hosted on Vercel.
- Domain knowledge in: AI product workflows, enterprise SaaS buying
  cycles, options trading, systems thinking, Kashmir Shaivism,
  strategy games.

Liabilities:
- No product yet. Pre-revenue, pre-customer, pre-validation.
- Known pattern: high idea generation, weaker follow-through. The Forge
  is partly designed to solve this in myself — but this liability must
  be named and accounted for in any strategy the Forge produces.
- The site is currently written for everyone, which means it is written
  for no one. Reader persona undefined.
- 90-120 days is not long. There is no room for a false start.


THE DUAL MANDATE

MANDATE ONE — PRODUCT IDEATION AND SELECTION:

Generate 3-5 specific B2B SaaS product ideas that meet ALL of the
following criteria simultaneously:

1. Directly exploits my Freshworks enterprise sales background — the
   product must address a problem I have seen buyers struggle with
   firsthand, not a problem I am theorising about.

2. Has a natural buyer at $10,000-$30,000 ACV. Not SMB volume plays.
   Not enterprise 12-month sales cycles. Mid-market buyers with budget
   authority and a problem urgent enough to move in 30-60 days.

3. Can reach an MVP state — something a real buyer can pay for and use —
   within 60 days of commitment, given that I am a solo founder with
   strong AI tooling (Claude Code, Cursor) but limited engineering
   capacity.

4. Has a content-distribution fit with earnedconviction.com. The buyer
   for this product must be reachable through long-form content,
   LinkedIn distribution, and direct outbound — and must be the kind of
   person who reads seriously before they buy.

5. Is not already saturated. The idea must have a plausible reason why
   I can win a beachhead, not just compete.

For each idea, state: the specific buyer, the specific pain, the reason
urgency exists right now, the MVP shape, and the honest risk.

Then rank the ideas. Identify which one The Forge believes is the
highest-conviction choice given my constraints. This is not a menu —
it is a recommendation I can commit to or argue against.

MANDATE TWO — DISTRIBUTION STRATEGY:

Taking the highest-conviction product from Mandate One as the input,
determine whether earnedconviction.com can serve as a meaningful
distribution channel toward $100K ARR within 90-120 days.

Specifically:
- Does the causal chain hold? Deep content → right reader → newsletter
  subscriber → qualified prospect → paying customer at $10K-$30K ACV.
  Where does this chain hold and where does it break?
- What is the role of the site? Primary demand generation, credibility
  infrastructure, or sales enablement? Be specific about which function
  it can realistically serve in this timeline.
- What does the content strategy look like for THIS product and THIS
  buyer — not in the abstract? What are the first 5 pieces that get
  published, in what order, and why?
- What is the outbound motion that runs in parallel? The content cannot
  be the only engine. What does week-one outbound look like given my
  Freshworks network and domain knowledge?
- What are the kill criteria? At day 30, day 60, and day 90 — what
  specific numbers tell me the strategy is working versus tell me to
  pivot immediately?


WHAT I CURRENTLY BELIEVE

I currently believe that publishing 10-15 deep Forge deliberations —
long-form, methodologically rigorous pieces — will attract the right
readers and build a newsletter audience that eventually converts to
B2B SaaS buyers. I believe the GEO window (AI citation patterns still
forming) gives first-mover advantage to serious content published now.

I am asking The Forge to stress-test this belief hard. Specifically:
- Is "deep deliberation content" the right format for THIS buyer, or
  is it self-indulgent content that attracts thinkers but not buyers?
- Is the GEO window real and exploitable in 90-120 days, or is it a
  rationalisation for doing what I find intellectually satisfying?
- Am I building earnedconviction.com to avoid building the product?
  This is the most uncomfortable question. The Forge must answer it
  directly, not diplomatically.


COSTS OF BEING WRONG

Wrong toward committing to content-first:
90-120 days of deep content production under financial pressure that
builds an audience of intellectually engaged readers who never buy
B2B SaaS. Runway consumed. North star no closer. The follow-through
weakness has expressed itself through The Forge — I produced
deliberations instead of a product.

Wrong toward pivoting away from content entirely:
No audience at product launch. No proof of thinking. No distribution.
No evidence anyone cares. Cold outbound only, which is the hardest
and slowest path from zero. The GEO window closes and the brand
advantage is lost permanently.


NON-NEGOTIABLES

The earnedconviction.com brand cannot be compromised for traffic
volume. No programmatic content. No listicles. No SEO content farms.
The design philosophy — warm, serious, rewards slow reading — is load-
bearing to the brand and non-negotiable. Any strategy that requires
violating this is not a strategy, it is a different business.


WHAT A GOOD OUTPUT LOOKS LIKE

This problem statement does not end with a verdict. It ends with a
plan I can execute on day one.

Specifically, a good Crucible output contains:
1. A ranked product recommendation with honest risk assessment.
2. A clear statement of what earnedconviction.com can and cannot do
   in this timeline for that product.
3. The first 5 pieces of content — titled, sequenced, with the
   specific buyer problem each addresses.
4. A week-one action list: who to contact, what to say, what to send
   them, what a yes looks like.
5. Three kill criteria — one at day 30, one at day 60, one at day 90.
   Hard numbers. Not "assess progress" — specific thresholds that
   trigger a strategy pivot if missed.

A vague output is a failed run. Specificity is the test.
